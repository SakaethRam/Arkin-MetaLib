#1

Recon & Enumeration ↗

Reconnaissance and Enumeration are key stages in cybersecurity, where attackers gather information about a target (reconnaissance) and actively probe the network or system to identify vulnerabilities and resources (enumeration). These steps help in understanding the attack surface for potential exploitation.

#2

Brute Force ↗

Brute force is a trial-and-error method used to crack passwords, encryption, or other security systems by systematically trying all possible combinations. It is simple but time-consuming, relying on computational power to succeed.

#3

DNS & Sub-Domain ↗

DNS and Sub-Domain attacks exploit vulnerabilities in the Domain Name System to redirect users to malicious websites or compromise subdomains of a target domain. These attacks can lead to data theft, website defacement, or unauthorized access to sensitive resources.

#4

Clickjacking ↗

Clickjacking is a malicious technique where attackers trick users into clicking hidden elements, like buttons or links, on a webpage without their knowledge. This exploits users into performing unintended actions, such as sharing sensitive information or authorizing harmful activities.
